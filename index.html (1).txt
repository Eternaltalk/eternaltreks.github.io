<h2>Everest Base Camp Trek Difficulty</h2>
<p>Spring season (March to June): The weather is favorable for trekking. This season attracts a lot of tourists, and the temperature reaches up to 25 degrees on summer days and drops down to -20 degrees at night.</p>


<p>Monsoon starts in July and lasts till mid-September. This time is not recommended for trekking as during this season, due to high rainfall the roads become more slippery and riskier.</p>


<p>Autumn (September to November) is also the best time for trekking in the Everest region. During this season, the entire region bloomed up with gorgeous rhododendrons and other wildflowers.</p>


<p>Winter begins from November to February. This season presents a very extreme cold. January is considered to be the coldest month of the year where the temperature falls and reaches to -36 degrees and can go down below to -60 degrees. Despite all the low temperature, the biggest issue faced by the mountain climbers is the hurricane, force wind, and wind chill where the force of the wind develop reaching more than 285 km/hr.</p>


<h3>Everest Base Camp Route</h3>
<p>Reaching Everest Base Camp is a dream for various trekkers. There are two base camps, the northern base camp in Tibet and the southern base camp in Nepal. Both are used by trekkers to ascend and descend where goods are carried by yaks, porters, and Sherpas.</p>


<p>The trails of the southern base camp are rough, rocky, and steep, making it challenging for trekkers. The route for the southern base camp starts from the Tenzing Hillary airport at Lukla (2,800 m), one of the most dangerous airports. From Lukla, the trek passes through picturesque villages like Phakding and Namche Bazaar, offering stunning views of Himalayan mountains.</p>


<p>From Namche Bazaar, the trail continues to Tengboche Monastery, Dingboche, Lobuche, Gorak Shep, and finally leads to Everest Base Camp (5,364 m).</p>


<h3>Everest Base Camp Difficulty</h3>
<p>The difficulty level of the Everest Base Camp trek is moderate. The trek typically takes around two weeks to complete. While prior trekking expertise is not required, trekkers should be determined and physically fit.</p>


<h3>EBC Trek Altitude</h3>
<p>Altitude is a major difficulty of the EBC trek, starting at 3,000 meters and reaching 5,400 meters at the base camp. Trekkers with breathing problems are advised to avoid this trek and seek medical advice before attempting it.</p>


<h3>Distance from Lukla to EBC</h3>
<p>The distance between Lukla and Everest Base Camp is approximately 130 kilometers. Despite the relatively short distance, trekkers should be prepared for the challenging terrain and high altitude.</p>


<h3>Training before EBC Trek</h3>
<p>Physical fitness is crucial for the EBC trek. Trekkers are advised to strengthen their leg muscles through exercises and practice hiking on rocky terrain to prepare for the trek's challenges.</p>


<h3>Safety Measures and Etiquette</h3>
<p>Before embarking on the EBC trek, trekkers should take precautions such as carrying basic medications, staying hydrated, avoiding alcohol, wearing appropriate trekking gear, and acclimatizing properly to prevent altitude sickness.</p>


<p>Additionally, trekkers should respect local customs and traditions, avoid standing in the streamside, be mindful of yak and mule traffic on trails, and refrain from giving money to begging children.</p>
